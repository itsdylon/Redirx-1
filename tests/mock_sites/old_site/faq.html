Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about working with TechCo Solutions. If you don't see your question answered here, please don't hesitate to contact us directly.

General Questions

What industries do you serve?

TechCo Solutions works with clients across a wide range of industries including financial services, healthcare, retail, manufacturing, telecommunications, and technology. Our diverse experience allows us to bring best practices and insights from multiple sectors to every engagement.

Do you work with startups or only enterprises?

We work with organizations of all sizes, from early-stage startups to Fortune 500 enterprises. We tailor our engagement models and pricing to fit your stage of growth and budget constraints.

Where are you located?

Our headquarters is in San Francisco, CA, with additional offices in New York and London. However, we work with clients worldwide and have successfully delivered projects across six continents. Many of our team members work remotely, allowing us to provide services in multiple time zones.

Services & Capabilities

What technologies do you specialize in?

Our team has expertise across a broad technology stack including Java, Python, JavaScript/TypeScript, .NET, cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud), mobile development (iOS, Android, React Native), and modern frameworks (React, Angular, Vue.js, Spring Boot). We stay current with emerging technologies and can adapt to your preferred tech stack.

Can you maintain existing applications or only build new ones?

We provide both new development and maintenance services. Many of our clients engage us to enhance, modernize, or support their existing applications. We're experienced at working with legacy codebases and can help you gradually modernize systems while maintaining business continuity.

Do you provide ongoing support after project completion?

Yes, we offer comprehensive support and maintenance services. All fixed-price projects include a post-deployment support period. We can also establish ongoing support agreements with defined service level agreements (SLAs) for production systems.

Process & Timeline

How long does a typical project take?

Project timelines vary significantly based on scope and complexity. A simple web application might take 8-12 weeks, while a complex enterprise system could require 6-12 months or more. During our initial consultation, we'll provide a realistic timeline estimate based on your specific requirements.

What is your development methodology?

We primarily use Agile methodologies, specifically Scrum and Kanban, which allow for iterative development, regular feedback, and flexibility to adapt to changing requirements. We work in sprints (typically 2 weeks) with regular demos and opportunities for course correction.

How involved will we need to be during the project?

We believe successful projects require active client participation. We'll need your involvement for requirements clarification, design approvals, sprint demos, and user acceptance testing. Typically, clients dedicate a product owner or project sponsor who commits 5-10 hours per week, with additional team members involved as needed for specific decisions.

Pricing & Contracts

How do you price your services?

We offer multiple pricing models including time & materials (hourly rates), fixed price projects, and dedicated team retainers. The best model depends on your project characteristics, budget constraints, and risk tolerance. See our pricing page for more details.

What payment terms do you offer?

For fixed-price projects, we typically structure payments around milestones (e.g., 25% upfront, 50% at key milestones, 25% upon completion). For time & materials engagements, we bill monthly in arrears. We're flexible and can discuss payment structures that work for your organization.

Do you require long-term contracts?

No, we don't require long-term commitments for most engagements. Time & materials contracts can be terminated with 30 days notice. Fixed-price projects have defined end dates. Dedicated team engagements typically have 3-6 month initial terms, after which they can be renewed monthly.
